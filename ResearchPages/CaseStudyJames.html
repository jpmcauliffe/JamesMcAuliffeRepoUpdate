This Case Study had an emphasis on:

  • The participation gap between racial backgrounds
    • “Children living in low-income neighborhoods are 20 to 60 percent more likely to be obese or overweight than children living in high socioeconomic status neighborhoods" (Hartman).
  • The participation gap between socio-economic statuses
    • “Many of the world's greatest soccer players of all time come from the dirt fields and poverty-like conditions, yet in the United States our professionals come from families that can afford to spend thousands of dollars a year on soccer clubs to get their children to higher levels of play” (Hartman).
    • "about 12 percent of parents overall saying that the cost of school sports caused a drop in participation rates for at least one of their children” (Hartman).
    • “About 28 percent of 13- to 17-year-olds from family incomes of less than $25,000 play team sports, and about 46 percent of that age group from households that make more than $100,000 play organized sports” (Hartman).
  • The participation gap between regional locations
  • Income inequality between racial/ethnic/geographic factors
